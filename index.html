About Future City

A global STEAM project where students engineer the next century.

Future City® is a 34-year global STEAM education program. Through project-based learning, students integrate science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics to design cities that respond to real sustainability challenges.

The program builds interdisciplinary thinking, complex problem-solving, civic responsibility, international perspective, and the confidence to explain ideas to experts.

Project-Based Learning

Teams explore real urban systems, identify constraints, and turn research into city designs.

Sustainable Thinking

Annual themes focus on climate, clean energy, waste, food systems, and resilient public services.

Expert Dialogue

Students present to educators and industry specialists, then refine ideas through Q&A.

International Exchange

Teams meet peers from different cities and cultures while sharing designs for a better future.

Inside The Showcase

Students build, test, present, and celebrate their future city ideas.

Future City is more than a review event. It is a collaborative design process supported by engineering societies, educators, mentors, schools, and families across China and the world.

City Design Division

One division, four ways to show the work.

The Summer Showcase includes the City Design Division for elementary and middle school teams. Teams communicate their process, solution, model, and presentation through the following deliverables.

Project Log

Document the team's research, decisions, tests, and progress.

Design Proposal

Present the future city concept and sustainability solution.

City Model

Build a tabletop physical model using recyclable materials.

Presentation & Q&A

Give a 7-minute presentation followed by 7 minutes of expert questions.
